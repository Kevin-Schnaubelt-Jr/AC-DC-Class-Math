The main reason for the US entering WWI, not including Germany's mistakes, I think, is British propaganda. What are you supposed to think about the war? We'll, what are your options? Britain, having a monopoly on war news, meant that the goings on of the war were whatever they said it was. The US was fed a steady diet of German atrocities, labeling the Germans as "The Hun." The US was also fed a steady diet of British heroism, and that the British were fighting for freedom. And British news was, of course, in English, which didn't help the Germans much either.

 Which brings us to the second main reason, America's strong English roots. The US was founded by the British, and as such many cultural aspects of the US are British. They shared a common language, common religious ideals, and even had a similar legal code. Most of British people, just as most American people, were WASPs, or "White, Anglo, Saxon, Protestants."

 The third reason why the US entered the war was that Americans were framing the war, or having it framed for them, as a democracy issue. The allies were made up of democracies with strong powers of representation, while the central powers were still mostly monarchies. The democracies that were there were in their infancy, and still had a lot of power in the hands of those monarchs.
