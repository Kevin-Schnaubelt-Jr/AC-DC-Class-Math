Huge reparations

 Germany had to pay an incredible amount of money and resources as recompense for the war. The reparations would also be continuous over a long period of time.

Lost German Territory

 German had to give up territory that was majority German, including parts of Poland, where many leaders were from.

Permanent Colonial Losses

 German lost all of it's colonies. This would likely not only remove their their status as a world power, but would hamper their ability to pay reparations.

Weak Military

 Germany was only allowed to have a military of 100k soldiers. In addition to this, they could not have tanks (ironic) and could only have a 15k man navy with no battleships. Also their air force was barred from using engine power. Essentially this would make them completely non competitive.

War Guilt Clause

 Signing the treaty of Versailles had a clause that put total blame on WW1 onto Germany. This coupled with everyone else, would likely seize up the state. But I guess in the end, Germany just didn't adhere to any of this anyway, and there was no effective system in place to enforce anything in the treaty.
