Literacy Behind Bars

 When Malcolm X describes a moment in prison where he, "never had been so truly free...", it's immediately evocative. The idea that a lack of literacy creates its own prison of ignorance is terrifying.

 Malcolm X's stance is that literacy is absolutely crucial to one's well-being, and at the time, a major point of pain of the African-American community, exacerbating an already relentless state of oppression.

 Malcolm X's narrative highlights the profundity of literacy. It cannot be overstated the importance of literacy as tool to assuage or overcome ignorance.
